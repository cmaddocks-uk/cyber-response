About this tool

The Cyber Incident Response Planner is a free, browser-based tool for UK schools and colleges. It is a companion to the DfE Digital Standards 2030 — Self-Assessment Tool, focusing specifically on the cyber response and recovery layer.

It is built and maintained by Christopher Maddocks, a former ANME Ambassador, as a contribution to the UK education community.

What it maps against

  • NCSC Incident Management — the structure of the plan (key contacts, severity, categorisation, escalation, playbooks, post-incident review) follows the NCSC's published guidance for organisations of any size.
  • DfE Digital Standards 2030 — Cyber Security core standard — questions reflect the DfE's stated expectations on risk assessment, training, MFA, response planning, asset registers and patching.
  • DfE Risk Protection Arrangement (RPA) Cyber Response Plan template — structure aligns with the impact assessment, communication templates and recovery event recording required by RPA cyber cover.
  • Ofsted Inspection Toolkit (November 2025) — Ofsted does not directly inspect cyber security, but several aspects of cyber response intersect with the safeguarding evaluation (Met/Not Met) and leadership and management areas. These cross-overs are flagged in the readiness check.

Privacy

This tool runs entirely in your browser. No data is sent to any server. There is no analytics, no cookies, no tracking. If you save your progress to a file, that file is created and downloaded directly by your browser. If you close the tab without saving, your data is lost.
